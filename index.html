// How I build

The principles didn't change. The factory did.

Five years ago, shipping meant a team of people grinding through discovery, pitch, build, release. In 2026 I ship at team velocity by orchestrating agents — I hold the taste, the judgment, and the why; a team of specialists does the rest, and reviews each other.

AI-native · 2026

A one-person software factory

I run Claude Code as a virtual product team — direct the work, let specialized agents execute and check each other before anything reaches a user.

01PlannerPressure-tests the idea and the why before a line gets written.
02ArchitectLocks the approach, the data model, the boundaries.
03DesignerKills AI slop and holds the bar on craft and taste.
04BuilderShips features in the right sequence, not the easy one.
05ReviewerFinds the production bugs a human skims right past.
06QADrives a real browser — not just the happy path.
07SecurityRuns the audits (OWASP / STRIDE) on every change.
08ReleaseOpens the PR, ships it, documents what changed.

The role I don't delegate: deciding what's worth building and whether what shipped is actually good. The agents are the team. I'm still the product.
